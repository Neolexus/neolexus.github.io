Epic Golfing Where the Map Turns Blank

By George Chapungu • Aug 24, 2025
If you think golf is all tartan pants and polite applause, let me take you somewhere wilder. I grew up believing Africa’s best stories started at sunrise and ended with stars. But even I didn’t imagine a golf course on the Makgadikgadi Salt Pans of Botswana—a stretch of white so vast it looks like you’ve landed on a different planet. We call it The Infinity Course. Here, there’s no fairway, no gallery, no rules but the ones you make. Just a single flag in the distance and you, a caddie from the San people reading the breeze, and a ball filled with wildflower seeds. Miss the shot? You’ve just planted hope for future zebras. Sink the putt? You’ve won the ultimate minimalist challenge...
